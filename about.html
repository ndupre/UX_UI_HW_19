Hi, I'm Nicole Dupré

Chilean-born UX Designer living in Brooklyn, NY since 2012.
My work focus on placing users at the center, by taking a data-driven approach to create seamless and meaningful interactions.

My Skills

User Research

Gain understanding of the users needs and painpoints is a key part my process, having bast experience designing User Research and User Testing.

Collaboration

As a consultant, collaboration, active listening, and flexibility have been key skills developed to bring ideas to life.

Prototyping

I’m skilled defining user flows, often taking them to quick paper prototypes and Lo-fidelity wireframes to test and iterate till defining Hi-fidelity mocks.

Experience

Graphic Designer graduated from the Design School of Universidad Católica of Valparaíso in 2008. Focusing on the study of Interaction Design and Information Architecture with an emphasis on visual languages as tools for collaborative creation.
Started my carreer by co-funding a Usability Studies and UI company, in the pursue of mentorship I left the venture and worked as a Junior UI designer at Betazeta networks. This lead me to join Estupendos, a collective of creative minds, where I participated in a wide range of projects while learning from my peers and developing my skills.
Later on, joined Multiplica, this allowed me to dive into Design Thinking and work on complex services and multichannel experiences. After migrating to the US, I continued this work as a contractor, working on projects for Brazil, Mexico, Chile, and the US.

Interests

The flexibility of my consultant work has given me the freedom to study food systems, become a Permaculture Designer, do organizing work at @GreeneAcres community garden. And for the past two seasons, to farm at the Brooklyn Grange, the biggest soil-based rooftop farm in the world. This year I grew literally thousands of plants that went to community gardens all around NYC!
I’m also the proud mama of an 11-year old that is the world’s most kind and creative person (I swear I’m not biased!) They make me think a lot about how to make the world less oppressive for children, so they can be their full selves and grow to be compassionate happy adults. For that we are part of The Brooklyn Freeschool community where I’m also a board member.
